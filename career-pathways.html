Career Pathways

The logistics roles we focus on

We work narrowly, on purpose. Here are the target roles we typically recruit for in NRW's logistics sector.

These are target roles based on current employer demand, not confirmed live vacancies. We label them this way deliberately. If you want to see genuine, currently open mandates, visit the Opportunities page.
Qualified Logistics Employment

Warehouse and logistics specialist roles

Fachlagerist (Warehouse Specialist)

Goods handling, stock control and warehouse operations. Entry via direct employment with relevant qualification, or via Ausbildung.

Fachkraft fur Lagerlogistik

A more advanced warehouse logistics qualification covering planning, systems and quality control, usually requiring prior vocational training.

Dispatch, Forwarding & Supply Chain

Coordination and planning roles

Logistics Coordinator / Disponent

Planning shipments, coordinating drivers and warehouse teams. Usually requires relevant experience and stronger working German.

Speditionskaufmann/-frau (Forwarding Agent)

Commercial and administrative side of freight forwarding. Typically requires a relevant qualification or demonstrable experience.

Technical & Operational Roles

Driving and equipment roles

Warehouse Equipment Technician

Maintenance of warehouse and material-handling equipment. Requires a technical qualification, usually mechanical or electrical.

Berufskraftfahrer (HGV / Truck Driver)

A distinct profession from forklift operation, requiring a relevant driving licence and qualification. We treat this as a separate role, not a variant of warehouse work.

Ausbildung Pathways

Training routes in logistics

Ausbildung is a structured, multi-year vocational training programme that ends in a recognised German qualification.

Ausbildung in Warehouse Logistics

Roughly three years, combining on-the-job training with vocational school, leading to a recognised Fachlagerist or Fachkraft qualification.

Ausbildung as Berufskraftfahrer

Vocational training toward a recognised HGV driving qualification, distinct from a basic driving licence.

An Important Distinction

Target roles are not automatically visa-eligible

A general warehouse-helper role, without a recognised qualification attached, does not normally meet the requirements of a skilled-worker visa route.

If your background fits a qualified role, an Ausbildung pathway, or a specific employer-sponsored route, we will explain exactly which one and why.
